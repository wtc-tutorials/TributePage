Malcolm X

Here’s a timeline of Malcolm X’s life:

  • Born Malcolm Little in Omaha, Nebraska.
  • His father, Earl Little, a Baptist minister and Garvey supporter, dies under suspicious circumstances.
  • Sent to foster homes after his mother is institutionalized. Leaves school after being told a law career isn't realistic for a Black man.
  • Sentenced to 10 years in prison for burglary. Learns the value of education and joins the Nation of Islam.
  • Paroled; meets Elijah Muhammad and becomes Malcolm X, symbolizing his lost African heritage.
  • Becomes a minister at Harlem Temple No. 7, gaining national attention for his powerful oratory.
  • Travels to the Middle East and Africa, connecting with global liberation movements.
  • Breaks with the Nation of Islam; forms the Muslim Mosque, Inc., advocating political and spiritual renewal.
  • Pilgrimage to Mecca changes his worldview; adopts the name El-Hajj Malik El-Shabazz and embraces unity among all races.
  • Travels widely across Africa, meeting anti-colonial leaders and pushing to bring African American rights before the UN.
  • Assassinated in New York City on February 21 at age 39 while addressing the Organization of Afro-American Unity.

If you’re not ready to die for it, put the word ‘freedom’ out of your vocabulary.

— Malcolm X

Malcolm X’s legacy endures as a symbol of courage, truth, and transformation. His evolving vision—from militant independence to global human rights—continues to inspire generations fighting for justice.
